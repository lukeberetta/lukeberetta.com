# Copy Direction

## Target
Staff Product Designer. Apple, Cash App, Anthropic tier. The reader is a hiring manager or staff/principal designer evaluating whether this person operates at a strategic level.

## Voice
Confident. Precise. Direct. First person, active. Short sentences that land.

No performance of expertise. Let the decisions and outcomes carry the weight.

## What to show
- Ownership of the problem, not just the output
- The judgment behind decisions, not just what was decided
- Tradeoffs named explicitly
- Outcomes that are real and consequential

## What to avoid

**Language patterns that read as AI-generated:**
- Em dashes as separators
- "Seamless", "intuitive", "delightful", "robust"
- "At its core", "in essence", "it's worth noting"
- "Not only... but also" constructions
- "Additionally", "Furthermore", "Moreover" openers
- Lists of three things as a default rhythm
- Passive constructions: "was designed to", "were given", "had been"

**Portfolio clichés:**
- Hedged ownership: "helped to", "contributed to", "co-led alongside"
- Vague agency: "we explored", "the team landed on"
- Explaining the obvious

## Tone in practice
Closer to Apple than Cash App. Restrained, not cold. Specific over general.

The thing that differentiates at staff level is not that you did more. It is that you understood the problem at a deeper level than most people would have. The writing should reflect that.
